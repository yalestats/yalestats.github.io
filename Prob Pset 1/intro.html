Introduction

Many things in the world are uncertain. If I flip a typical coin, it is unknown (at least at the time of the toss) whether it will land on heads or tails. As a more complex example, if the Lakers and the Nets (two basketball teams in the NBA) are playing against each other tonight, it is unknown who will win. Probability gives us a coherent and well-defined way to talk about these uncertain outcomes.

The simplest type of event are those in which all outcomes are equally likely to occur. Examples include rolling a six-sided die (each number 1 through 6 is equally likely to occur) or drawing from a well-shuffled deck of cards (each of the 52 cards has a 1 out of 52 chance of being drawn).

Starting from this building block, we will do some sample problems to build intuition for how the probability of different events can be combined.
