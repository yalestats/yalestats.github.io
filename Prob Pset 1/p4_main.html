We are trying to decide if we should bring an umbrella with us on our way to class. From experience living in New Haven, we have an idea of the chance of rain based on the temperature during the midday.

There is a 40 percent chance that the midday temperature will be below 65 degrees and a 60 percent chance that it will be above 65 degrees. If the temperature is below 65 degrees, we think that there is a 50 percent chance that it will rain. If it is above, we think that there is a 25 percent chance it will rain.

What is the probability that it rains?

Here, we are interested in the total probability that it rains. That means the outcomes could either be (below 65 AND rains) or (above 65 AND rains). We need to combine our knowledge of formulas for P(A AND B) and P(A OR B) to figure out how to compute the probability of this event.
