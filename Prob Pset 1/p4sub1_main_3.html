In finding the probability of drawing a marked ball, we've essentially found the probability of rain from the previous problem! What steps did we follow to do this?

First, we found the probability of every outcome. We started with the unconditional probabilities we were given (whether it was a warm and cold day) and used the formula P(A AND B)=P(A)P(B|A) to find the probability of every event. For example, we wanted to find the probability P(cold AND rain), and we did so using P(cold AND rain)=P(cold)P(rain|cold). We did the same for all combination of cold/hot and rain/no rain. We did all of this implicitly when we calculated what combination of blue/red and marked/unmarked our 20 balls should be.

Second, we determined which of the four outcomes met the conditions we want ("Is it raining") and used the formula P(A OR B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A AND B). In this case, event A was "it is a cold day and it rains" and event B was "it is a warm day and it rains". Note that P(A AND B) = 0 because it cannot be both a warm and cold day.
