Note that while we’ve (hopefully) built a lot of intuition for how means and standard deviations move under constant multiplication and addition, we’ve left out one small detail which is that a normal distribution remains normal under these transformations. For the purposes of this course, it is sufficient to simply know that this is true without worrying about why.

Here we see how to use our rules to convert a standard normal distribution into any normal distribution we like, as long as we know the mean and variance. We can do the reverse as well. By subtracting by the correct number and dividing by the correct number, we can convert any normal distribution into a standard normal (which can then be looked up in our standard normal table). This is the approach we must take when solving problems involving probabilities of normal distributions!
