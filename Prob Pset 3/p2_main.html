Problem 2: Normal Distributions cont.

Given that demand exceeds 220 units, what is the probability that you missed out on at least 20 units worth of additional sales? (That is that demand exceeded your stock by at least 20 units).

Recall that demand is normally distributed with mean 200 and standard deviation 15.

This is an interesting problem that asks you to compute a conditional probability (you can tell that this is a conditional probability problem by the phrase "Given that demand exceeds 220 units" which invites you to assume that we are in a situation where we know that demand is larger than 220). In this case, this is the probability that you lost at least 20 units worth of sales given that you ran out of stock. How would you write this in conditional probability form? Can we use our knowledge of conditional probability formulas to manipulate this into a statement (or combination of statements) that we know how to calculate?
