Problem 3: Normal Distributions cont.

Suppose you want to ensure that the probability of running out of stock is 1 percent or less. How many units should you stock?

Recall that demand is normally distributed with mean 200 and standard deviation 15.

If you arrived at this answer, you probably relied on a heuristic such as the 99.7 percent rule to guess that 3 standard deviations above the mean would be sufficient. For this problem, you should calculate the precise number of units that are required.

If you arrived at this answer, you probably looked up the wrong number in the probability table, likely because you forgot that the probability table gives the probability for the area to the left of the cutoff. Here, we want to make sure that the area to the right contains less than 1 percent of the probability. This answer also does not pass a smell test (which you should always perform before committing to a quantitative result).
