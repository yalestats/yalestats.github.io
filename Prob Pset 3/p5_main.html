An analyst sees your new plan and informs you that two of the three locations served by the hypothetical warehouse are located very close to each other. Close enough that customers often end up deciding between the two, so that a high demand shock at one location might suggest a low demand shock at the other (because all the customers have gone to the first). The analyst estimates that the covariance between demand at these two locations is -90. (Demand at the third location is independent of the other two).

Recall that demand in the three locations is distributed normally with mean 200 and standard deviation 15. Given the new information above, what is the lowest number of units that can be stocked at the warehouse while keeping the probability of running out of stock below 1 percent?
