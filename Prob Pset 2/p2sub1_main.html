Sub Problem: Conditional Probability

Conditional expectations combine the logic of conditional probability and expected values. In the same way we could ask “what is the probability that the Lakers (an NBA team) win given that LeBron James scores more than 30 points?”, we can also ask “what is the expected number of points that the Lakers score given that LeBron James scores more than 30 points?”.

Once we’ve thought about conditional expectations this way, it’s clear where the conditional expected value formula comes from. We compute expected values by taking the sum of all possible outcomes multiplied by the probability that each outcome occurs. Thus, we compute conditional expected values by taking the sum of all possible outcomes multiplied by the conditional probability that the outcome occurs.
