Problem 4: Probability Calculations and Expected Values

Adrian is listening to music on a playlist that he likes but does not love. Every time he finishes a song, there is a 15 percent chance that he will stop listening before the next song plays (he will listen to the first song for sure). After 5 songs, he will stop listening with certainty.

What is the expected number of songs that Adrian will listen to?

This is a similar calculation to the previous expected value question in the problem set, so consider starting there if you skipped it. The main difference here is that the probabilities associated with outcomes are substantially more difficult to compute. We recommend drawing a probability tree. Because Adrian will listen to one song with certainty, this first “branch” of the tree will involve a 0.15 chance that he will stop there and the outcome will be 1 and a 0.85 chance that he will continue listening and a similar branch will follow.
