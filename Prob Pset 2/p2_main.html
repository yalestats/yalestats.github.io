Problem 2: Expected Values cont.

Suppose you are told that you will be meeting with Negotiator A. Now what is your expected payment?

Recall that you think there is a 25 percent chance they will accept your offer of $10 million, a 40 percent chance they will counteroffer $15 million which you will accept, and a 35 percent chance that they will counteroffer something higher which you will deny (and spend $0 million in this transaction).

This problem asks you to compute a conditional expectation. In this case, it is the expected value of the transaction given that you meet with Negotiator A. A conditional expectation calculation looks a lot like a normal expectation calculation, but with the probabilities replaced with conditional probabilities.
