One approach to this problem would be to follow the same approach as the last problem and compute the conditional expectations directly using the conditional probability formula. However, in this situation, we can exploit the intuition that conditional expectations and probabilities involve “stepping into” the probability tree.

We are told that it rains. Therefore, we can “step forward” into our probability tree and head down the first fork (which is whether it rains or not) towards the direction of rain. We can then use probabilities from this “inner tree” to compute our conditional expectation!
