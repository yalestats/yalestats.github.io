The numbers are all so large because the variance is inherently in “square” units of whatever we are working with. In this problem, we are working in units of millions of dollars, so the variance is described in millions of millions of dollars. Because a million dollars is a big unit, these mechanically leads to a massive variance, even if the spread of the data is small. Thus we often use the standard deviation, which is the square root of variance, and is described in units that are comparable to the mean.
