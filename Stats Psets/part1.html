Topic 1: Population Parameters and Sample Estimates

We are often interested in estimating population parameters using sample data. The population parameters are features of what statisticians call a data generating process.

The most commonly used population parameter is the population average \( \mu \). The population average for a roll of a fair die is the expected value of a die roll \(\mu = \frac{1+2+3+4+5+6}{6} = 3.5\).

Suppose we have 2 different sample of 10 rolls of a fair die: {4, 1, 6, 2, 5, 4, 1, 3, 6, 2} and {3, 5, 2, 6, 6, 3, 4, 2, 3, 5}. What are the sample averages in these samples? You should get \(\frac{4 + 1 + 6 + 2 + 5 + 4 + 1 + 3 + 6 + 2}{10} = 3.4\) and \(\frac{3 + 5 + 2 + 6 + 6 + 3 + 4 + 2 + 3 + 5}{10} = 3.9\).

Notice that neither of the sample averages above is equal to the population average of 3.5. Despite this, we use sample averages like the ones above to estimate population averages when population averages are unknown. The same is true for other population parameters.
