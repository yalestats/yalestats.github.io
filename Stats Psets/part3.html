Question 1: Stating Hypotheses

Does a change in a website's design increase conversion rates? Are investment bankers paid more than software engineers?

When we collect sample data on the salaries of investment banking analysts and software engineers or the conversion rates on a website, we do not observe the population parameters that we want to know about. Instead, we must use our sample data to construct estimates of population parameters like true average earnings or true average conversion rates.

The goal of any hypothesis test is to make an inference about an unknown population parameter (e.g. true average earnings) using our sample data. When we perform a hypothesis test, the alternative hypothesis \(H_1\) is our new theory about the world, the null hypothesis \(H_0\) is the old view, and we seek evidence that might allow us to reject the old view in favor of our new theory.
