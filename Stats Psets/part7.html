Question 1: Introduction to Regression

Suppose the data generating process for salaries \(Sal\) at a specific firm is given by $$Sal = \beta_0 + \beta_1 Exp + u$$ where \(Exp\) is the years of experience for a particular employee and \(u\) is a random variable that also affects employee salary.

Central question: given data on \(Exp\) and \(Sal\), how can we estimate \(\beta_0 \) and \(\beta_1 \)?

If we are willing to assume that (1) \(E[u|Exp] = E[u]\) and (2) \(E[u] = 0\), we can use OLS! The first assumption (1) is known as conditional independence and implies that any other variables that affect salary (e.g. ability) for employees of the firm aren't systematically related to the years of experience that worker has at the firm.

The second assumption (2) is just a normalization.
